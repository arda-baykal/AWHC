Proposed Outcome Categories & How to Measure Them

When your staff record a result from an event or case, they'll select from these categories. We've included proposed indicators so you can track progress over time.

Short-term Outcomes (Influence)

Categories:

  • Increased awareness of prevention-focused practices
  • Increased awareness of systems planning approach
  • Increased awareness of early intervention practices
  • Knowledge mobilization capacity built
  • Culture of continuous learning established
  • Youth engagement practices documented

How You Measure:

  • # of stakeholders reporting increased awareness (survey)
  • Commitment Curve Survey scores
  • Key informant interview findings
  • % satisfaction rate (target: 80%+)
  • # of service provider network members engaged
Medium-term Outcomes (Leverage)

Categories:

  • Increased uptake of prevention-focused policies
  • Increased uptake of systems planning approach
  • Increased uptake of early intervention practices
  • Greater sector capacity to address youth homelessness
  • Youth meaningfully engaged in decision making
  • Policy agenda informed by Learning Community

How You Measure:

  • # of organizations adopting new practices
  • # of communities implementing systems planning
  • # of policy recommendations acted upon
  • Commitment Curve Survey — uptake scores
  • Case studies of practice change
  • # of Indigenous-led partnerships (target: 5+)
Long-term Outcomes (Impact)

Categories:

  • Systems transformation to prevention-focused approach
  • Prevention and reduction of youth homelessness
  • Improved well-being of youth, families and communities
  • Reduction in length of time youth experience homelessness

How You Measure:

  • # of policies changed (laws, regulations, funding structures)
  • # of new prevention programs established
  • # of communities with prevention-focused systems
  • # of practices shifted from reactive to preventive
  • Average length of shelter stay (decrease)
  • Policy tracking — changes in government approach
  • Community-level case studies of transformation

Your System

What You'll See in Mareto

Five pages in the sidebar. Each one is simple. Here's what you can do in each:

Page What You Track Example
People Anyone AWH interacts with — first name, last name, email, phone, their function (Elder, Board Member, etc.), and which organization they belong to Mark Aston, Covenant House Toronto, Partner Contact
Organizations Any organization AWH works with — name, type (can select multiple: Government, Indigenous Led, etc.), Canadian or International designation Covenant House Toronto — Service Provider, Child Welfare
Events Everything your team does — type, date, who was there, which organization, part of which case, part of which program, and the outcome achieved HF4Y Training for Oklahoma Health Dept — Training Delivery, linked to Heidi, linked to TTA Program
Cases Ongoing engagements — type (Initiative, Research, etc.), status (Active, Closed), which program it belongs to, who's involved Edmonton Systems Planning Engagement — Active, Systems Planning Collective Program, Capacity Building
Programs Structured offerings AWH delivers — name, type, start/end dates, which organization funds it HF4Y Training — TTA Program, funded by Making the Shift

When you log an event, you connect it to the people, organizations, cases, and programs involved. Mareto then uses these connections to show your impact — how many trainings led to how many outcomes across which programs.
